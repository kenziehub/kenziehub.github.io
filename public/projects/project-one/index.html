Buddy Challenge Feature Design

Project Summary
#

Buddy Challenge Feature Design was a student project created in collaboration with Jenna Emperador and Colvin Wong. Over three weeks, we explored how Buddy could better support users after onboarding by introducing a feature that makes budgeting feel more motivating, structured, and rewarding over time.

The final concept was a new Challenges section designed to turn budgeting into a more engaging and goal-driven experience. By giving users short-term wins, clearer progress, and a stronger sense of momentum, the feature aims to help Buddy improve retention beyond initial account setup.

Context
#

  • Project details: Student project designed in collaboration with Jenna Emperador and Colvin Wong
  • Timeline: 3 weeks
  • Role: UX Researcher, UX Designer, Mobile App Designer
  • Tools: Figma
  • Constraints: N/A

The Challenge
#

Buddy has a post-onboarding retention problem: users are willing to try the app and connect their financial accounts, but many stop engaging because they do not feel motivated to continue budgeting. The experience does not yet create enough ongoing value, clarity, or encouragement to turn initial setup into a lasting habit.

Brief
#

The brief was to design a new feature for Buddy that would introduce a fresh persona-driven experience and create stronger long-term motivation for users. The goal was not just to help people start budgeting, but to make them want to keep coming back to the app.

Goals
#

  • Increase post-onboarding engagement by making budgeting feel more rewarding.
  • Help users build momentum through visible progress and short-term achievements.
  • Reduce the sense that budgeting is difficult, time-consuming, or purely manual.

Process
#

Research
#

Through competitor analysis and intended user interviews, we found that the biggest challenge was not getting users to download the app or connect their bank accounts and cards, but getting them to keep using it afterward. Many users described budgeting as complicated, time-consuming, and difficult to maintain, especially when it requires ongoing manual effort and offers little immediate reward.

Even after setup, users often lacked a clear sense of progress, motivation, or long-term value. Budgeting started to feel like a chore rather than a helpful habit, which made it easy to disengage. This showed that Buddy needed to create more ongoing encouragement and a clearer feeling of achievement if it wanted to sustain retention.

Strategy
#

Based on this research, we focused on designing a feature that reframed budgeting as a more active and motivating experience. Rather than relying only on tracking and account connection, we explored how challenges could introduce structure, encouragement, and a sense of accomplishment.

The concept centered on helping users work toward manageable short-term goals while making their progress visible. This direction aimed to turn budgeting from a passive responsibility into something more approachable and habit-forming.

Design Development
#

We developed the feature as a dedicated Challenges section within the Buddy app. The experience was designed to guide users into simple challenge flows, show progress clearly, and reinforce continued engagement through goal completion.

Wireframes and wireflows were used to map how users would discover, join, and complete challenges within the product. This helped us refine how the feature could fit naturally into the existing mobile app experience while keeping interactions simple and motivating.

Solution
#

We designed a Challenges section to make budgeting feel more engaging, motivating, and goal-driven. The feature gives users a clearer reason to return to the app by offering short-term wins, visible progress, and a stronger sense of accomplishment as they work toward financial habits.

Instead of treating budgeting as a static tracking task, the solution supports continued use through guided participation and encouragement. This makes the overall Buddy experience feel more active, rewarding, and sustainable after onboarding.

Outcome
#

  • Designed a feature concept aimed at improving Buddy’s retention after onboarding.
  • Created a challenge-based experience that supports motivation through progress and short-term wins.
  • Produced wireframes and wireflows that demonstrate how the feature would work within the mobile app.

Reflection
#

This project reinforced how important motivation is in financial product design. A budgeting app does not only need to function well at setup. It also needs to create enough clarity, value, and encouragement for users to stay engaged over time.

What went well was identifying a focused retention problem and designing a concept that directly addressed it through behavior and habit formation. If the project continued, the next step would be testing whether the challenge system genuinely increases engagement and determining which types of rewards or progress cues are most meaningful to users.

Final Overview
#

Buddy Challenge Feature Design shows how a relatively small product feature can address a larger retention issue when it is grounded in user motivation. By introducing a Challenges section, we created a concept that helps budgeting feel more manageable, rewarding, and worth returning to.
